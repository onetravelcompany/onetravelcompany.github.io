Baku City Tour aims to showcase the key attractions and architectural landmarks of Baku, painting a luxurious image of the city. Explore both modern and ancient facets of city life through this tour.

Highlights

Nohur Lake

Nohur Lake, formed post-WWII to provide water to nearby villages, boasts pristine waters and stunning scenery. It's a popular tourist spot with well-established facilities around Nohur-Gol. The reservoir supports aquaculture and rowing, with ice cover reaching 40-50 cm in winter. Ice formation begins in late December, usually thawing by late March, but timing varies yearly due to weather fluctuations.

On the opposite side of "Yanardag" another hill used to burn for thousands of years, but as its natural gas leakage moved on due to tectonic shifts the three flames ceased to burn a few hundred years ago. Nevertheless, this fire is believed to have inspired the symbol of Baku which features three flames and the sea. Around the Absheron Peninsula more of these burning rocks used to exist and were used as sacred places by Zoroastrians in ancient times.The larger Yanardag reserve offers more heritage sites such as an active mud volcano, a natural sulfur spring, the Girmaki valley and others which can be visited with one of our tourist guides.

Yeddi Gozel Waterfalls

Yedi Gozel Falls, also known as the Seven Beautiful Waterfalls, resides in Gabala city, a renowned tourist spot and the country's tallest falls at 53 meters above sea level. It cascades in seven stages, with only two visible from below. To witness all seven, visitors must ascend stairs, requiring some physical exertion. Yet, the reward is a captivating panorama. Amidst the dense, sun-shielded forest, the falls offer a cool, shadowed ambiance. Adventure enthusiasts will find a restaurant and tea house nearby, adding to the allure of this natural wonder.

Tufandag Mountain resort

Azerbaijan's two ski resorts in the Caucasus Mountains offer a refreshing change for skiing enthusiasts, doubling as summer and winter retreats. Tufandag, just 4 kilometers from Gabala city center, boasts 12 kilometers of slopes suitable for all skill levels. State-of-the-art snowmaking technology ensures optimal conditions, while cable cars provide stunning views. Accommodation options include luxury and upper-mid level hotels, with a ski school available for beginners. In summer, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, paragliding, and jeep tours, complementing the resort's winter allure.

Summer Activities at Tufandag
  • Hiking: Explore the scenic trails and lush landscapes surrounding Tufandag.
  • Cycling: Take on thrilling mountain bike trails for an adrenaline-packed adventure.
  • Paragliding: Soar through the sky and take in panoramic views of the mountains and valleys below.
  • Jeep Tours: Embark on guided jeep tours to discover hidden gems and remote areas.
  • Nature Walks: Immerse yourself in nature with leisurely walks, enjoying the fresh mountain air and diverse flora and fauna.
  • Cable Car Rides: Ascend to higher peaks via the cable car to enjoy breathtaking vistas of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Relaxation: Unwind and rejuvenate at the resort's amenities, including spa facilities and scenic spots for relaxation.
